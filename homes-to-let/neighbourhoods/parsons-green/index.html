The once Fulham Football Club ground, ‘Parsons Green’ is now well known as a great summer spot to sit on the green with a jug of Pimm’s and to watch the world go by.

The vibrant green being spoilt for choice with its upmarket pubs, The White Horse, Duke on the Green, and Aragon House a truly social spot. The area captures a unique village atmosphere, whether it be strolling through the charming streets of Victorian houses or meeting a friend at one of the many independent brunch spots Hally’s, Boys N Berry's, or Ella’s place. Being well-known for offering great local produce, from Emili’s delicatessen, Flavourtown bakery, Bygram an organic refill market, and of course Parson’s Nose one of London’s best butchers.

Parsons Green not only has picturesque parks, but it also offers great local amenities catering for all ages whether it’s hiring a public tennis court, visiting one of many independent fitness studios from Hot Yoga to spinning, or spending an afternoon in the local library.

The area has real appeal for young professionals and families with all amenities on their doorstep and being a stone’s throw away from central London.
